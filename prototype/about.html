Scott A. Cole

About & Books

AI Strategy Consultant. Dual MBA. A few decades of mostly relevant experience. The person and the publications behind this demo.

I started out doing antivirus research at McAfee — heuristic detection, byte-code work. Got a NATAS award for helping resolve a polymorphic virus that had spread to a lot of machines. Good place to learn that being wrong has real consequences.

A decade running small businesses

Before consulting, I co-founded massmusic.com and sold it to AudioHighway.com in 1999. After that I spent about ten years actually running small businesses — a construction and design company, a real-estate investment firm, a handful of fix-and-flip projects along the way. Not advising owners on decisions like the ones in this app. Making them, with my own money on the line.

Back to AI

I was brought in to fix an underperforming ML system at Change Healthcare — took prediction accuracy from 88% to 98%, and had to build the governance around it to keep it compliant. Since then, GenAI strategy work for a few larger clients, and co-founding an AI-driven risk-assessment platform for insurance.

Also

Columbia MBA, Berkeley Haas MBA, a computer science degree from UC Davis before that. I've written a number of books along the way, including a series on how executives can make good AI decisions without needing to become technical. This app — governance checks, a Monte Carlo range instead of a single confident-looking number, a human sign-off on every recommendation — is more or less that argument, built.

"Profitable AI—not just competitive capability—is the only valid metric for enterprise deployment. Focus on the architecture, secure the infrastructure, and build for intent."
